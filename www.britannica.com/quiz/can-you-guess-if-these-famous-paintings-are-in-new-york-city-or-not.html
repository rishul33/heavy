Can You Guess If These Famous Paintings Are In New York City Or Not?

Question: Washington Crossing the Delaware (1851), Emanuel Leutze
Answer: You can see La condition humaine (1933), René MagritteEmanuel Leutze’s Washington Crossing the Delaware (1851) at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City.
Question: Meeting an Evening on a Road (1889), Fritz Syberg
Answer: You can’t see Fritz Syberg’s Meeting an Evening on a Road (1889) in New York City. You have to pay a visit to the Nationalmuseum in Stockholm, Sweden, to catch a glimpse.
Question: Madame X (1883–84), John Singer Sargent
Answer: You can see John Singer Sargent’s Madame X (1883-1884) at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City.
Question: St. Joseph and the Christ Child (late 17th–18th century), Cuzco School
Answer: You can see St. Joseph and the Christ Child (late 17th–18th century), a Peruvian painting typical of the Cuzco School, at the Brooklyn Museum in New York City.
Question: The Skater (1782), Gilbert Stuart
Answer: You can see Gilbert Stuart’s The Skater (1782) at the National Gallery of Art in Washington, D.C.
Question: Queen Anne (c. 1685), Willem Wissing
Answer: You can only find Queen Anne  (c. 1685), by Willem Wissing, at the National Galleries of Scotland.
Question: La condition humaine (1933), René Magritte
Answer: René Magritte’s La condition humaine (1933) can be viewed at the National Gallery of Art in Washington, D.C.
Question: Pennsylvania Station Excavation (c. 1907–08), George Wesley Bellows
Answer: George Wesley Bellows’ Pennsylvania Station Excavation (c. 1907–08) is on display in the Brooklyn Museum in New York City.
Question: Lady at the Tea Table (1885), Mary Cassatt
Answer: You can visit Mary Cassatt’s Lady at the Tea Table (1885) at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City.
Question: At Breakfast (1898), Laurits Andersen Ring
Answer: You can’t find Laurits Andersen Ring’s At Breakfast (1898) in New York City. To see it, you have to go all the way to the Nationalmuseum in Stockholm, Sweden.
Question: Mrs. Walter Rathbone Bacon (1897), Anders Zorn
Answer: You can see Anders Zorn’s Mrs. Walter Rathbone Bacon (1897) at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City.
Question: Woman (c. 1952), Willem de Kooning
Answer: Woman (c. 1952), by Willem de Kooning, I s located at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City.
Question: Pad No.4 (1947), Stuart Davis
Answer: You can find Stuart Davis’s  Pad No.4 (1947) in the collection of the Brooklyn Museum in New York City.
Question: The Kiss (1859), Francesco Hayez 
Answer: You can find Francesco Hayez’s The Kiss (1859) at the Pinacoteca di Brera in Milan, Italy, along with these other works.
Question: The Judgment of Paris (c. 1528), Lucas Cranach the Elder
Answer: You can find The Judgment of Paris (c. 1528) by Lucas Cranach the Elder at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City.
