Monsters, Ghouls, and Ghosts Quiz

Question: Who is the first ghost to visit Ebenezer Scrooge in A Christmas Carol?
Answer: The ghost of Jacob Marley, Scrooge’s business partner, is the first ghost to visit Ebenezer Scrooge in A Christmas Carol.
Question: Who is the author of The Haunting of Hill House, a novel about a house rife with supernatural horrors?
Answer: Shirley Jackson wrote The Haunting of Hill House in 1959. This novel, about a house rife with supernatural horrors, furthered her reputation as a master of psychological suspense.
Question: What U.S. state has horror writer Stephen King set many of his novels and short stories in?
Answer: Stephen King has set many of his novels and short stories in the state of Maine.
Question: What hotel is Jack hired as caretaker of (and later possesses him) in Stephen King’s The Shining?
Answer: Jack is hired as caretaker of the remote Overlook Hotel in Stephen King’s The Shining.
Question: In Bram Stoker’s Dracula, what does the monster allegedly need from his home country to stay healthy?
Answer: In Bram Stoker’s Dracula, Dracula is in possession of 50 boxes of dirt from his home country, which he allegedly needs to stay healthy.
Question: Which inventor also produced the first Frankenstein film?
Answer: The first Frankenstein film was produced by Thomas Edison in 1910.
Question: How long did it take Anne Rice to write her first novel, Interview with the Vampire?
Answer: Anne Rice wrote her first novel, Interview with the Vampire, in only five weeks.
Question: In The Legend of Sleepy Hollow by Washington Irving, what is thrown at Ichabod Crane that he mistakes for a severed head?
Answer: In The Legend of Sleepy Hollow by Washington Irving, a pumpkin is thrown at the protagonist Ichabod Crane, which he mistakes for a severed head.
Question: Which is a seminal short story regarding witchcraft written by Nathaniel Hawthorne?
Answer: “Young Goodman Brown” is a seminal short story regarding witchcraft written by Nathaniel Hawthorne. It follows a young Puritan realizing that his wife is a member of a coven of witches.
Question: Which narrative popularized the theme of the female vampire?
Answer: “Carmilla” by Sheridan Le Fanu is a classic story that popularized the theme of the female vampire.
Question: What filmmaker’s work largely developed the modern zombie?
Answer: The work of American filmmaker George A. Romero, including Night of the Living Dead, largely developed the modern fictional zombie.
Question: Which foreboding novel with an unnamed narrator inspired a resurgence of Gothic romances?
Answer: Rebecca by Daphne du Maurier inspired a resurgence of Gothic romances after its publication. The novel follows its unnamed narrator as she navigates her status as the second wife of a rich man whose first wife, Rebecca, seems to still be haunting their home and lives.
